inventory_2 Product Settings Guide

The Product Settings screen serves as the central hub for managing everything related to your catalog of items and services in the Invoicey application. From structuring your items to configuring applicable taxes, you can control the core aspects of what you sell.

Here is a detailed explanation of each field and option available on the Product Settings screen:

1. Categories

"Group your products logically"

The Categories section allows you to organize your inventory by defining logical groups (e.g., Electronics, Services, Clothing).

  • Purpose: Helps keep your product catalog neat and structured.
  • Usage: When creating a new product, you can assign it to a category, making it easier to filter, search, and generate categorized reports later on.

2. Tax Configuration

"Set GST rules & rates"

The Tax Configuration section is where you manage the taxes applicable to your business.

  • Purpose: Ensures that the correct tax amounts are automatically calculated and applied when generating quotations and invoices.
  • Usage: You can define different tax slabs (such as 0%, 5%, 12%, 18% GST). Once defined, these tax rates can be easily selected and linked to individual products or services in your catalog.

3. Product Master

"Manage your item catalog"

The Product Master is your main inventory and service database.

  • Purpose: This is where you actually create the items you sell.
  • Usage: You can add new products or services by specifying details like the item name, description, unit price, stock keeping unit (SKU), and link them to the categories and taxes you defined earlier. You can also edit or delete existing items here.

4. Discounts

"Promo codes & coupons"

The Discounts section enables you to create and manage promotional offers for your customers.

  • Purpose: Helps drive sales and customer loyalty through structured promotional campaigns.
  • Usage: You can create custom discount rules such as flat amount discounts or percentage-based discounts. These can be applied as promo codes or coupons directly to quotations and invoices during checkout.

Best Practice: We recommend setting up your Tax Configuration and Categories before adding items to the Product Master. This way, you can smoothly assign the correct taxes and categories as you create new products without having to jump back and forth between screens.
