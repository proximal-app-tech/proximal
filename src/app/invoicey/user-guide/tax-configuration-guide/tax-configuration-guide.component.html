account_balance Tax Configuration Guide

The Tax Configuration screen allows you to define the various tax rates and rules applicable to your business. Setting these up accurately ensures that taxes are automatically calculated and applied correctly on your invoices and quotations.

Here is a detailed breakdown of each field on the Tax Configuration screen:

1. Tax Rule Name

Required

This is the display name for your tax rule, which will help you identify it when assigning taxes to products or services.

  • Purpose: To provide a clear label for the tax rate.
  • Usage: Enter a descriptive name like GST 18%, VAT 5%, IGST 12%, or Zero Rated. This name will appear in dropdowns across the application when selecting applicable taxes.

2. Tax Rate (%)

Required

The actual percentage amount for the tax rule.

  • Purpose: Defines the calculation percentage used to determine the tax amount on an item's base price.
  • Usage: Enter the numerical percentage value (e.g., 18.0 for 18%). Do not include the % symbol, just the number.
info
GST Logic & Automatic Splitting: The system is designed to handle Indian GST smartly. During billing, the system will automatically look at the customer's state and your business's state:
  • Intra-state (Same State): The tax rate will be automatically split into CGST and SGST (e.g., an 18% rule becomes 9% CGST and 9% SGST).
  • Inter-state (Different State): The tax rate will be applied as a single IGST charge (e.g., 18% IGST).
lightbulb
Best Practice: After creating your tax rules here, you can assign them to individual items in the Product Master. Setting up your taxes before adding your inventory will save you significant time.
