request_quote Quotation - User Guide

Overview

The Quotation feature allows you to generate professional price estimates and proposals for your customers before finalizing a sale. Quotations do not deduct items from your active inventory and are saved separately from actual invoices. Once a customer approves a quote, it can easily be converted into a real invoice.

Creating a Quotation

Creating a quotation follows a nearly identical process to creating a standard bill, with a few key distinctions tailored for providing estimates.

1. Customer Details

  • Look up or Add Customer: Enter the 10-digit phone number to autofill details, or enter new customer information.
  • Accuracy is Key: Ensure the address, State, and SEZ status are correct so the estimated GST calculation accurately reflects what the final invoice will look like.

2. Adding Items to the Quote

  • Select Products/Services: Add items the customer is inquiring about.
  • Edit Pricing: If you are offering a special negotiated rate for this proposal, tap on the unit price of any item to edit it. The system will still enforce your minimum price rules to ensure the quote remains profitable.
  • Inventory Handling: Adding products to a quotation does not reduce your current stock levels. The stock is only deducted if the quote is later converted into an invoice.

3. Discounts and Taxes

  • Apply Discounts: You can apply global or item-level discounts to sweeten the deal for your prospective customer.
  • Tax Preview: The quotation will provide a full breakdown of the taxes (CGST, SGST, or IGST) based on the customer's state, giving them a transparent view of the final expected cost.

4. Saving and Sharing the Quote

  • Quotation Number: Quotes are generated with a unique "QT" prefix (e.g., QT12345) to clearly distinguish them from official invoices.
  • Save Quotation: Once generated, the quote is saved in your Quotation List.
  • Share: You can print the quotation or share it as a PDF via email or messaging apps with your customer.

Converting a Quote to an Invoice

When a customer accepts your quotation:

  1. Navigate to the Quotation List.
  2. Select the approved quote.
  3. Click on the option to Convert to Invoice.
  4. The system will automatically load the Billing View with all the customer and product details from the quote pre-filled.
  5. You can make any final adjustments, proceed to payment, and generate the final tax invoice. At this point, product stock levels will be officially updated.
